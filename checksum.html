FAQ

What is CRC?

Cyclic Redundancy Check (CRC) is an error-detecting code. A polynomial division is performed on the data to produce a fixed-size checksum that can detect common transmission errors.

What is the difference between CRC-8, CRC-16, and CRC-32?

They differ in the polynomial size and detection capability. CRC-8 produces 8-bit checksums, CRC-16 produces 16-bit, and CRC-32 produces 32-bit. Longer CRCs detect more error patterns.

What is a parity bit?

A parity bit is the simplest error detection method. Even parity makes the total number of 1s even, odd parity makes it odd. It can detect single-bit errors but not correct them.
