FAQ

What are bitwise operations?

Bitwise operations work on individual bits of binary numbers. AND gives 1 only when both bits are 1, OR gives 1 when either bit is 1, XOR gives 1 when bits differ.

What is bit shifting?

Left shift (SHL) moves bits to the left and fills with 0s, effectively multiplying by 2 for each position. Right shift (SHR) moves bits right, dividing by 2.

Where are bitwise operations used?

Bitwise operations are fundamental in embedded systems, networking (subnet masks), graphics programming, cryptography, and low-level hardware manipulation.
