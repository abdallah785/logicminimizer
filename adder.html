FAQ

What is a full adder?

A full adder is a combinational circuit that adds three 1-bit inputs (A, B, Carry-in) and produces a Sum and Carry-out. Multiple full adders are chained to create multi-bit adders.

What is ripple carry?

In a ripple carry adder, the carry output of each full adder is connected to the carry input of the next. The carry "ripples" through all stages, which limits speed but simplifies design.

How does binary subtraction work?

Binary subtraction A-B can be done by adding A to the two's complement of B. Set Carry-in to 1 and invert all bits of B.
