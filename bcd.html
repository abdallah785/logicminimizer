FAQ

What is BCD?

BCD (Binary Coded Decimal) represents each decimal digit with a 4-bit binary code. Unlike pure binary, each group of 4 bits can only represent 0-9, making it easy to convert to and from decimal displays.

How is BCD different from binary?

In BCD, each decimal digit is encoded separately in 4 bits. For example, 259 in BCD is 0010 0101 1001 (three groups). In pure binary, 259 would be 100000011 (9 bits total).

Where is BCD used?

BCD is used in financial calculators, digital clocks, 7-segment displays, and any system where exact decimal representation is needed without rounding errors.
