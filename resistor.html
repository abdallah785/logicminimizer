FAQ

How do you read resistor color codes?

For a 4-band resistor: Band 1 and 2 are significant digits, band 3 is the multiplier (number of zeros), and band 4 is tolerance. Read from the side closest to a band.

What is the difference between 4-band and 5-band resistors?

5-band resistors have three significant digits instead of two, giving more precise values. The extra band is added before the multiplier.

What does the tolerance band mean?

Tolerance indicates the accuracy of the resistor value. Gold means ±5%, silver ±10%, and brown ±1%. A 100 Ohm ±5% resistor can actually be 95-105 Ohms.
